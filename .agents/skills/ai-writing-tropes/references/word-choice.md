# Word Choice Tropes

## "Quietly" and Other Magic Adverbs

Overuse of "quietly" and similar adverbs to convey subtle importance or
understated power. AI reaches for these to make mundane descriptions feel
significant.

Also includes: "deeply", "fundamentally", "remarkably", "arguably".

Bad:

- "quietly orchestrating workflows, decisions, and interactions"
- "the one that quietly suffocates everything else"
- "a quiet intelligence behind it"

Fix: Remove the adverb. If the thing is important, the facts will show it.

## "Delve" and Friends

"Delve" went from an uncommon English word to appearing in a huge percentage of
AI-generated text. Part of a family of overused AI vocabulary.

Also includes: "certainly", "utilize", "leverage" (as a verb), "robust",
"streamline", "harness".

Bad:

- "Let's delve into the details..."
- "Delving deeper into this topic..."
- "We certainly need to leverage these robust frameworks..."

Fix: Use plain words. "Look at", "use", "strong", "simplify".

## "Tapestry" and "Landscape"

Ornate or grandiose nouns where simpler words work. "Tapestry" for anything
interconnected. "Landscape" for any field or domain.

Also includes: "paradigm", "synergy", "ecosystem", "framework".

Bad:

- "The rich tapestry of human experience..."
- "Navigating the complex landscape of modern AI..."
- "The ever-evolving landscape of technology..."

Fix: Say what you mean. "The field of AI" or just "AI". "How things connect"
instead of "tapestry".

## The "Serves As" Dodge

Replacing simple "is" or "are" with pompous alternatives. AI avoids basic copulas
because its repetition penalty pushes it toward fancier constructions.

Also includes: "stands as", "marks", "represents".

Bad:

- "The building serves as a reminder of the city's heritage."
- "Gallery 825 serves as LAAA's exhibition space for contemporary art."
- "The station marks a pivotal moment in the evolution of regional transit."

Fix: Use "is". "The building is a reminder of the city's heritage." Shorter,
clearer, better.
