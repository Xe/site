I don't know if I like working at higher levels of abstraction

Working with generative AI means that we often operate at a higher level of abstraction than we normally do. It's natural it happens. I don't know if I like this new level of abstraction.

This new level of abstraction is weird. A lot of it is focused on getting things done rather than focusing on the quality or craft in the process. Sure, with a lot of of this stuff and a lot more numerously productive than I usually am. I'm just wondering about how much of the soul is lost in the process.

When I write I try to make people feel something. My goal is a writer is to make you have emotions when you read what I write. You, undo a lot of this new stuff involving generative AI, a lot of the hard things get sanded down. Everything becomes homogenous. Things become the numerical average of what they should be. This is not good for making people feel things.

Sure you can't actually make people feel things that you use a flow like this. I've done that recently. But I don't know, it just feels like we're losing something in this transition.

I'm going to try to keep this voice of mine when I'm talking to you as an equal. I feel that's been one of the most effective parts of my style. I'm just really wondering how to square this with all of the things that are happening in our industry.

I know people that are trying to break into this industry as juniors and honestly I don't know how to help them. We are an industry that is normally focused on quality and craft yet somebody can just Yolo something out and get hired by Facebook for it.

It feels like this part of the industry is marked by a bunch of double think and nuance and opinions that don't fit into tweets. Maybe there is something there with artificial intelligence tools. Maybe there isn't. Maybe I'm in some kind of AI psychosis right now. Maybe I'm not. I don't know.

What I do know is that I'm reusing these tools that I am in order to investigate where the bar is. I want to see what's possible, and maybe seeing what's possible requires me to use some pretty expensive tools.
