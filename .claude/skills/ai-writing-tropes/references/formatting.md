# Formatting Tropes

## Em-Dash Addiction

Compulsive overuse of em dashes for dramatic pauses, parenthetical asides and
pivot points. A human writer might use 2-3 per piece naturally; AI will use 20+.

Bad:

- "The problem -- and this is the part nobody talks about -- is systemic."
- "The tinkerer spirit didn't die of natural causes -- it was bought out."
- "Not recklessly, not completely -- but enough -- enough to matter."

Fix: Use commas, parentheses, or separate sentences. Reserve em dashes for the
one or two places where they genuinely earn their keep.

## Bold-First Bullets

Every bullet point or list item starts with a bolded phrase or sentence. Common in
Claude and ChatGPT markdown output. Almost nobody formats lists this way when
writing by hand. A telltale sign of AI-generated documentation and blog posts.

Bad:

- "**Security**: Environment-based configuration with..."
- "**Performance**: Lazy loading of expensive resources..."

Fix: Write list items as normal sentences. If the list needs headings, use actual
headings.

## Unicode Decoration

Use of unicode arrows, smart/curly quotes, and other special characters that can't
be easily typed on a standard keyboard. Real writers typing in a text editor
produce straight quotes and `->` or `=>`.

Bad:

- "Input -> Processing -> Output"
- "This leads to better outcomes -> which means higher engagement"

Fix: Use `->`, `=>`, or just write "leads to". Use straight quotes.
